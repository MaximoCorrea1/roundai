# Vision

## The problem

Only **28% of Argentine adults are financially literate**. For most people, "investing" is abstract, intimidating, or feels like something for someone else. Saving by willpower fails — discipline is the wrong tool for a recurring habit. And goals stay vague: "ahorrar" is not a thing you can see yourself getting closer to.

So the money that *could* compound just sits in a checking account losing to inflation, or gets spent, because nothing turns the intention into an automatic, understandable action.

## The wallet's pain

Digital wallets won the daily-spend layer, but they're stuck on the value layer. Balances are transactional, not invested — **low TVL**. Users churn to whatever app has the latest feature — **low retention**. And the core feature set (transfers, QR pay, a debit card) is **commoditized**: everyone has it, nobody owns it.

The wallet needs a reason for money to *stay*, and a reason for users to come back. It doesn't have a financial-education engine, and building a trustworthy AI coach in-house is expensive and off-mission.

## The bet

**Round-up + AI coach = AUM and retention for the wallet, automatic and understandable investing for the user.**

- Every payment quietly sweeps a calibrated margin into the user's goal. No discipline required — the habit is the rail.
- A coach that already knows the user makes it understandable: it reads their liquidity, proposes a *sustainable* margin, and projects an honest timeline to a concrete goal.
- For the wallet, parked-and-growing money is **AUM that doesn't churn**, and a coach users talk to is **a reason to come back**.
- For the user, investing becomes the default — a thing that happens *while* they live, toward something they can actually see.

## The B2B thesis

roundai is **embedded, not standalone**. The client is the wallet; the end users are the wallet's users. The wallet keeps its brand, its rails, its relationship — roundai is the layer it plugs in to turn spending into investing. We don't compete with wallets for users; we make wallets stickier and more valuable, and we ride their distribution to the millions of people who most need a financial coach and will never go looking for one.

The contrast *is* the pitch: a deliberately neutral host wallet, and a memorable roundai layer dropped inside it. That's exactly what an integration looks like.
